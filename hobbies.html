I LOVE RAIN

aka meteorology

I really really like the randomness of the weather and never knowing for sure what's going to happen, it makes me feel comfortrable knowing the world isn't figured out yet.

Even with the best forecast models (my favourite is HRRR) which need supercomputers to run, the most we'll ever know is about 2 weeks into the future.

Because the Risk of Rain is very low where I live, I always need to be ready in case it does, and knowing meteorology helps a lot with it.

Linux

ACTUALLY got into it when trying WSL on my laptop, and liking it a LOT. Did you know it's faster to cross compile a Tauri app for Windows on Linux than it is to just compile it on Windows? Also an entire Ubuntu instance somehow launches faster than POWERSHELL.

I distro-hopped a lot less on my laptop, because I actually used it and didn't like deleting all my data every time. But I still distro-hopped quite a bit, going from Manjaro (with Sway), to Omarchy (which sucked), to installing Arch manually and using hyprland.
