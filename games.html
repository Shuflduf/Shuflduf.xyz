Piracy

If you want to support the original creators directly, piracy obviously is not the answer.

Personally, I've gotten more people into these games BECAUSE I was able to play them for free. I would not have payed $70 for Risk of Rain 2 (and all the DLCs), but I've gotten at least 5 people to buy the base game (about $150 total).

If I were ever to publish a game with a price tag on it, I would also upload it somewhere it can be accessed for free by anyone, as I wouldn't want anyone to miss out on what I made. It's not like they would've payed if it wasn't available anywhere else, right?

My Recommendations

Risk of Rain 2

I fucking love Risk of Rain 2. I have no idea where I'd be without Risk of Rain 2. Risk of Rain 2 is the culmination of everything good in this world. It is pure and loving.

How can you look at this and tell me this wasn't the best thing ever created.

But for real though, whenever I have spare time this is usually my first pick. Each run is wildly different, and I don't even expect to win most of them. I'm currently doing Eclipse 4, which means I'm almost halfway done through the hardest gamemode ever.

The lore of the game is also absurdly well-written, because from the gameplay one would assume all the effort went into that. Most of it is written in logbook entries detailing a certain monster or environment while also giving information about the bigger picture, which also means it's all optional for those who are only interested in the gameplay. If you don't want to navigate the logbooks manually, you can watch this video by The Grovetender

You can it here or here (for free).

Risk of Rain Returns

While it doesn't have the emotional appeal of something like Risk of Rain 2, it does win in terms of gameplay polish. Every single little action and interaction feels so intentional, the love put into the game is just yelling at you throughout the entire run. The UI is so sexy I made a linux rice after it

How can you look at this and tell me this wasn't the second best thing ever created.

I honestly don't play it as much as I should, mostly due to the aforementioned Risk of Rain 2, but I still play it constantly on my laptop when I don't have a mouse (like at school). It's a lot less buggy than Risk of Rain 2, and since it's 2D it also performs better late-game. Both are really really really good and I recommend both of them over anything else.

You can it here or here (for free).

UNBEATABLE

This one is definetly more personal, I doubt it had an bigger effect on anyone than on me.

I started playing the demo soon after it came out, and I liked it a lot for the gameplay, so I followed development until release. I honestly didn't give it that much thought, I just knew it would be a damn good rhythm game.

When it came out, I remember opening it and crying in the fucking intro sequence. The place where you select your difficulty made me more emotional than any other game could (excluding Risk of Rain 2 of course).

I don't want to spoil anything, as it is a very story-focused game, and not something you can experience multiple times, so it'd mean a lot to me if you played it.

You can it here or here (for free).

Deltarune

Similarly to UNBEATABLE, this one might not apply to everyone the same way it did to me.

I remember a friend of mine got me really into Undertale, and while I'm no longer in contact with them, I've stayed in the community ever since. But I still only started Deltarune when chapter 2 already came out.

TLDR: It's really damn good.

I remember playing through everything multiple times just to find everything, and practicing the bosses in order to no-hit them.

When chapters 3 and 4 came out, I was at school, and so I was unable to focus on any of my classes due to Deltarune.

Once again to avoid spoilers, I don't want to spoil anything major, but there were some scenes that resonated with me I don't think anything else could've (except UNBEATABLE).

You can it here or here (for free).
